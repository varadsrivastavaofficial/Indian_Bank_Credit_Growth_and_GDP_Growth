# Indian Bank Credit Growth and GDP Growth (India, 2000–2024)

This repository contains graphical analysis and supporting material for a macroeconomics project examining the relationship between bank credit growth and GDP growth in India over the period 2000–2024.

---

## 📌 Project Overview

The study investigates how variations in bank credit influence economic growth in India. Using time-series data, it analyses trends, correlations, and simple regression results to understand the strength and nature of the relationship between credit expansion and GDP growth.

The project is part of a Macroeconomics course (ECO 204) at IISER Bhopal.

## 📈 Key Findings

* Strong positive correlation between bank credit growth and GDP growth (r ≈ 0.72).
* Credit growth explains nearly 52% of GDP growth variation.
* A 1% increase in credit growth is associated with ~0.31% increase in GDP growth.
* Non-food credit is the primary driver of economic activity.
* Post-2020 recovery shows synchronized growth in both credit and GDP.

These results highlight the importance of credit availability in sustaining economic growth.

---

## 🧠 Methodology

The analysis is based on:

* **Graphical Analysis** , to identify trends and co-movements
* **Correlation Analysis** , to measure strength of association
* **OLS Regression Model**

Regression model used:

$$
GDP_t = \alpha + \beta \cdot Credit_t + \varepsilon_t
$$

## ⚠️ Limitations

* Uses a bivariate model (excludes inflation, interest rates, etc.)
* Correlation does not imply causation
* Does not account for structural breaks (e.g., COVID-19, demonetisation)
* Assumes linear relationship
